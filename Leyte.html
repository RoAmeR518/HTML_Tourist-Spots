Leyte is a province of the Philippines located in the region of Eastern Visayas. Tacloban city is its capital and occupies 75 percent of the northern part of the island of Leyte. Leyte is located in the west of samar, north of southern Leyte and south of Biliran. To the west of Leyte beyond the Sea Camotes, the province of Cebu is located.

The traveler Ruy Lopez de Villalobos first arrived on the island in 1543 and named the place as Las Islas Felipinas

The battle of Leyte Gulf occurred in the waters surrounding the island from October 23 to 26, 1944. It was the largest naval battle in modern history, where an estimated 212 American ships fought with the 60 ships of the Empire of Japan, including the warships of Yamato and Musashi.

The first battle took place in Leyte on October 20,1944. A successful conquest of the island's Alysans took place and became an integral part of the ultimate victories of the Filipino and American forces in the Philippines.
